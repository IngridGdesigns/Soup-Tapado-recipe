Tapado Seafood soup

Tapado is a delicous seafood soup. It's a popoular recipe from the Caribbean coast of Guatemala Belize and Honduras. It's mainly eatened by the Garifuna communities in the region. (Click here to learn more) Tapado is full of fresh fish, shrimp, plantains and yummy coconut milk soup broth.

Photo Credit: http://www.sercano.com/tapado-de-mariscos/

Ingredients:

  • 1 Tbsp garlic clove
  • 2 plum tomato's
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 tbs of fresh oregano
  • 1 tbsp fresh basil
  • 1 tbsp fresh oregano
  • A handful of fresh cilantro
  • 3 peeled and sliced plaintains (make sure you sli (ce them diagnal and not too thin)
  • 1 green banana sliced diagnal
  • 20 raw medium size shrimp peeled and deveined but tails on
  • 2 lbs of white fish
  • 1 lb. of clams
  • 3 whole crabs
  • Salt to taste
  • Black Pepper to taste
  • 2 brown coconuts or 2 cans of coconut milk and/or cream
  • 2 cups of Seafood or Chicken stock
  • (Optional) 1/1 red bell pepper
  • (Optional)Cheesecloth
  • Coconut oil or Vegetable oil
  • A medium to large soup pot

Instructions:

  1. If you can, make fresh coconut milk! (Otherwise use 2 cans of coconut milk.) To make it from scratch, click here.
  2. Clean and prepare you seafood: Devein and clean your shrimp in a butterfly cut, remember to leave shrimp tails on. Clean your crab, clams and remove fish scales, slicing fish to fillets. Set aside in the fridge.
  3. Chop the plaintain and the bananas diagnol. Set aside on a plate.
  4. Place enough Coconut oil or Vegetable oil to coat your soup pot
  5. Chop the onion, tomato and garlic and red pepper (optional). Sauté until tender but do not brown.
  6. Add the coconut milk and sliced plantains to pot and bring to a light boil, in which to the broth should turn to a creamy orange pinkish color.
  7. Add the fresh oregano, cilantro and basil. Add salt, pepper, and the Seafood/Chicken stock to taste.
  8. Simmer for at least 30 minutes or until plantains are tender like a cooked potato
  9. Grab your fish fillets and after lightly salting and peppering your fish fillets, sauté them in a large frying pan with Coconut oil or vegetable oil for about three minutes on each side or until cooked through.
  10. Once the plantains in the broth are tender, add the fish fillets and any other seafood and cook for just a few more minutes.
  11. Remove from heat. Serve into individual bowls with ladle. Serves 6 bowls of Tapado...or 4 very big bowls.
